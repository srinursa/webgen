Causes of Acid Rain

Acid rain can be caused by water vapour dissolving with one of a variety of different oxides, such as SO2 (Sulfuric Dioxide) and N2O (Nitrogen Dioxide).

These gases are emitted from various sources such as factories, cars, and power plants.

Sulfur Dioxide

Sulfur dioxide reacts with water to produce sulfurous acid, which reacts with oxygen to produce sulfuric acid, which is a form of acid rain. This is represented by the word equations below:

Sulfur dioxide + Water → Sulfurous Acid
Sulfurous Acid + Oxygen → Sulfuric Acid

Represented as chemical equations, these reactions are:

SO2 (g) + H2O (l) → H2SO3 (aq)
H2SO3 (aq) + ½O2 (g) → H2SO4 (aq)

Sulfuric acid usually lowers the pH of rain to below 5.0.

Nitrogen Dioxide

Nitrogen dioxide reacts with water to produce nitric acid and nitrous acid, of which nitric acid is a component of acid rain. This is represented by the word equation below:

Nitrogen dioxide + Water → Nitric acid + Nitrous Acid

Represented as a chemical equation, this reaction is:

2NO2 (g) + H2O (l) → HNO2 (aq) + HNO3 (aq)
